Project Context

About Montefiore Square

This community park in Harlem features an open lawn with terraced stone seating, a garden with perennial plants, and a paved plaza for events, markets, and performances.

The redesign focuses on making Montefiore Square more welcoming, comfortable, clean, shaded, and active for the Harlem community.

Existing Conditions

Before

Limited greenery and minimal shaded areas.

Hard surfaces dominate the plaza with little comfort.

Limited seating options and lack of flexible space.

The space lacks activity and inviting features.

Proposed Redesign

After

The area was improved with flowers, benches, and trees, making it look more welcoming.

New trash and recycling bins were added to keep the space clean.

Shade from trees makes the area more comfortable for people.

Stores and shops were added, bringing more life and activity to the space.

Design Moves

Final Redesign Idea

01 / Greener Space

Flowers, trees, and planting areas make the plaza feel softer, brighter, and more welcoming.

02 / Clean & Comfortable

Trash bins, recycling bins, benches, and shade help people feel more comfortable staying in the space.

03 / More Activity

Small shops and gathering areas bring more neighborhood energy, movement, and community life.

Community Impact

Highlights

Montefiore Square supports community events, public gathering, and neighborhood activity. The redesign makes the park more useful for relaxing, walking, meeting friends, and enjoying Harlem.
