Project Overview

Montefiore Plaza in Harlem has the potential to become more than a walkway. Our proposal transforms the plaza into a safe, active, green, and culturally meaningful public space.

Main idea: A successful plaza should not be measured only by how expensive it is. It should be measured by how well it serves the people who use it every day.
$18.4M

Original city investment used as a comparison point.

$13.6M–$14.2M

3 Phases

Safety first, environmental upgrades second, cultural activation third.

All Ages

Our Redesign Proposal

Green Infrastructure

Add native trees, rain gardens, shaded seating, and permeable pavement.

Flexible Seating

Use movable chairs, modular benches, and community tables.

Harlem Culture

Murals, music, poetry, history displays, and public art.

Small Business Support

Vendor kiosks, food spaces, and local weekend markets.

Safety by Design

Lighting, visibility, and increased foot traffic.

Technology Access

Wi-Fi, charging stations, and digital accessibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is this redesign important?

It improves accessibility, safety, environmental quality, local business opportunities, and community gathering space.

How could this cost less?

The proposal uses phased construction, modular systems, sustainable materials, and partnerships.

Will the plaza support local businesses?

Yes. Vendor kiosks, markets, and event spaces help support Harlem businesses.
