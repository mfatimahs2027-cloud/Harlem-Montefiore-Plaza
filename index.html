Montefiore Square Park Redesign

Harlem community park improvement proposal

Manhattan • Mahnoor Fatima & Shamor Johnson

Montefiore Square is located at Broadway, Hamilton Place, and West 138th Street. This redesign improves safety, greenery, usability, and community identity.

About the Park

Montefiore Square Park Redesign focuses on transforming a mostly pass-through space into a welcoming neighborhood destination that supports daily life, culture, and local business.

  • Open lawn with terraced stone seating
  • Garden with perennial plants
  • Paved plaza used for events, markets, and performances

Location

Broadway, Hamilton Place, and West 138th Street, Harlem, Manhattan.

The redesign improves safety, greenery, usability, and community identity while supporting local businesses and community events.

Users & Needs

Users

  • Local residents: shade, seating, cultural design
  • Commuters: clear paths, quick-use space
  • Businesses: benefit from foot traffic
  • Visitors: need directions and identity

Problems

  • Pedestrian conflicts
  • Seating not flexible
  • Poor lighting
  • Mostly pass-through space

Why This Matters

The transformation of Montefiore Plaza brings social and environmental benefits by creating a welcoming space for community activities, improving safety, and supporting local businesses. Added green infrastructure enhances air quality, reduces heat, and manages stormwater, making the plaza more sustainable and improving overall quality of life.

Montefiore Plaza’s transformation includes major infrastructure upgrades such as new water, sewer, and drainage systems to prevent flooding and improve stormwater management. Proper grading and underground systems help control rainwater, especially around the terraced lawn.

Additional utilities support lighting, irrigation, and public amenities, while strong foundations ensure durability.

The long-term success depends on maintenance costing about $90,000 per year, covering sanitation, horticulture, and repairs.

  • Sanitation: cleaning and trash collection
  • Horticulture: plant care, watering, pruning
  • Infrastructure repairs: paths, lighting, seating safety

Overall, ongoing maintenance supports sustainability and keeps the plaza functional for the community long-term.
