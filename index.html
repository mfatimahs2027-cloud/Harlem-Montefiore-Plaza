Montefiore Square

Harlem community park improvement proposal

By Mahnoor Fatima and Shamor Johnson

Manhattan • Mahnoor Fatima & Shamor Johnson

Montefiore Square is located at Broadway, Hamilton Place, and West 138th Street. This redesign improves safety, greenery, usability, and community identity.

Users & Needs

Who uses Montefiore Square and what they need from this space.

Users

  • Local residents: shade, seating, cultural design.
  • Commuters: clear paths and safe circulation.
  • Businesses: increased foot traffic and visibility.
  • Visitors: orientation, identity, and welcoming spaces.

Problems

  • Pedestrian conflicts and unclear routes.
  • Inflexible, limited seating.
  • Poor lighting and safety perception.
  • Plaza used mainly as a pass-through, not a destination.

Before & After

From hardscape pass-through to a green, active community plaza.

The redesign introduces more greenery, flexible seating, and safer circulation. It transforms Montefiore Square into a place where people can gather, rest, and participate in community life instead of just passing through.

Budget & Projected Impact

How funds are focused and how the redesign supports public and economic growth.

  • Shade sails — $30k–$55k
  • Moveable seating — $15k–$25k
  • Vendor market zone — $25k–$45k
  • Wayfinding signs — $7k–$13k
  • Lighting — $10k–$20k
  • Total Cost: $87k–$158k
