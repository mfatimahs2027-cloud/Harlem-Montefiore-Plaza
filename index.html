About Montefiore Square

This community park in Harlem features an open lawn with terraced stone seating, a garden with perennial plants, and a paved plaza for events, markets, and performances.

Users & Problems

Users

Local residents: Need culturally relevant design, bilingual signage, shade, and accessible seating.

Commuters: Need clear pathways and shade for short transit use.

Business owners: Benefit from increased foot traffic and vendor space.

Visitors: Need wayfinding and a strong neighborhood identity.

Problems

Safety: Pedestrian conflicts at crossings.

Seating: Fixed granite seating lacks flexibility.

Lighting: Poor visibility at night.

Usage: Space is mostly pass-through, not active.

Before

Limited greenery and minimal shaded areas.

Hard surfaces dominate the plaza.

Limited seating options.

Low activity and engagement.

After

Improved with greenery and seating.

Added trash and recycling bins.

More shade for comfort.

More shops and activity.

Why This Matters

The transformation of Montefiore Plaza brings social and environmental benefits by creating a welcoming space for community activities, improving safety, and supporting local businesses. Added green infrastructure enhances air quality, reduces heat, and manages stormwater, making the plaza more sustainable and improving overall quality of life.

Montefiore Plaza’s transformation includes major infrastructure upgrades such as new water, sewer, and drainage systems to prevent flooding and improve stormwater management. Proper grading and underground systems help control rainwater, especially around the terraced lawn. Additional utilities support lighting, irrigation, and public amenities, while strong foundations ensure durability. Overall, these improvements create a safe, sustainable, and functional public space.

The long-term success of Montefiore Plaza depends on consistent operations and maintenance to keep the space clean, safe, and functional. The estimated annual cost is about $90,000, covering sanitation, horticulture, and infrastructure repairs.

Sanitation includes trash collection and regular cleaning to maintain a welcoming environment. Horticulture maintenance involves caring for trees, grass, and planted areas through watering, pruning, and seasonal upkeep. Infrastructure repairs ensure that pathways, seating, lighting, and irrigation systems remain safe and functional.

Overall, ongoing maintenance supports sustainability, preserves the quality of the plaza, and ensures it continues to serve the community effectively over time.
