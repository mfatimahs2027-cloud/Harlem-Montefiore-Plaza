Project Context

About the Space

Montefiore Square is a community park in Harlem with an open lawn, terraced stone seating, perennial gardens, and a paved plaza for events, markets, and performances.

The redesign focuses on making the space more welcoming, shaded, clean, comfortable, and active for the Harlem community.

Existing Conditions

Before

Limited greenery and minimal shaded areas.

Hard surfaces dominate the plaza with little comfort.

Limited seating options and lack of flexible space.

The space lacks activity and inviting features.

Proposed Redesign

After

The area was improved with flowers, benches, and trees, making it look more welcoming.

New trash and recycling bins were added to keep the space clean.

Shade from trees makes the area more comfortable for people.

Stores and shops were added, bringing more life and activity to the space.

Design Moves

Final Redesign Idea

01

Greener Space

Flowers, trees, and planting areas make the plaza feel softer, brighter, and more welcoming.

02

Comfort

Benches, shade, and cleaner spaces help people stay longer and enjoy the park.

03

Activity

Small shops and gathering areas bring more movement, energy, and community life.
