Project Overview

Montefiore Plaza in Harlem has the potential to become more than a walkway. Our proposal transforms the plaza into a safe, active, green, and culturally meaningful public space.

Main idea: A successful plaza should not be measured only by how expensive it is. It should be measured by how well it serves the people who use it every day.
$18.4M

Original city investment used as a comparison point.

$13.6M–$14.2M

Projected redesign estimate using phased construction and partnerships.

3 Phases

Safety first, then environmental improvements, then cultural and economic activation.

All Ages

Designed for children, teenagers, adults, seniors, and people with disabilities.

Our Redesign Proposal

Green Infrastructure

Add native trees, rain gardens, shaded seating, and permeable pavement.

Flexible Seating

Use movable chairs, modular benches, and community tables.

Harlem Culture

Include murals, music, poetry, history displays, and public art.

Create vendor kiosks, weekend markets, and food spaces for local businesses.

Safety by Design

Use better lighting, clear sightlines, active programming, and more foot traffic.

Technology Access

Provide free Wi-Fi, charging stations, QR menus, and accessible audio information.

Frequently Asked Questions for Residents, Visitors, and Investors

Why is this proposal better than the current redesign?

It adds culture, programming, food access, Wi-Fi, seating, shade, safety, local business opportunities, and long-term maintenance planning.

How could this cost less than $18.4 million?

The proposal uses phased construction, modular furniture, sustainable materials, partnerships, and community-supported operations.

How will investors benefit?

Investors may benefit from increased foot traffic, vendor revenue, sponsorship visibility, and stronger neighborhood activity.

Where would revenue come from?

Vendor permits, event sponsorships, seasonal markets, small business partnerships, grants, and nonprofit funding.

How will food ordering work?

People can order through QR codes, walk-up counters, large-print menus, or accessible audio menu systems.

Will the plaza have free Wi-Fi?

Yes, the proposal includes free public Wi-Fi with accessibility support.

How will the plaza be safe at night?

Smart lighting, clear sightlines, active programming, vendor presence, and increased foot traffic.

How will seniors use the plaza comfortably?

Shaded seating, benches with backs, smooth walkways, large-print signs, and quiet seating areas.

How will children use the plaza safely?

Soft play areas, family seating, stroller-friendly paths, colorful ground art, and safe walking zones.

How will blind or low-vision users access the space?

Tactile paving, audio descriptions, high-contrast signs, large-print maps, and screen reader-friendly web pages.

What are the biggest risks?

Funding gaps, construction disruption, maintenance challenges, and overcrowding during events.
